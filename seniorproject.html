Tufts Cognitive and Brain Science Senior Project 2014
For my senior project, I conducted research in the Human Computer Interaction Lab at Tufts University. I specialized in brain computer interfacing using Functional Near Infrared Spectroscopy (fNIRS) , a non-invasive measure of blood oxygenation levels in the frontal lobe of the brain. We integrated Google Glass , a wearable computer with an optical display, and prototyped an application which utilizes fNIRS-data to make intelligent adaptations in the Glass interface.
Over the course of the year, I was responsible for programming the front-end application created with Unity3D. I also spear-headed the pilot testing once we had the Glass device integrated with the Unity application. This is still a work in progress, and the PhD students at the HCI lab at Tufts are continuing to move forward with this exciting research.
